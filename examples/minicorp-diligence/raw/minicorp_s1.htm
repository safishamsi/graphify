FORM S-1 REGISTRATION STATEMENT

MiniCorp Inc.

Filed: January 15, 2024

PROSPECTUS SUMMARY

MiniCorp Inc. ("MiniCorp" or the "Company") is a technology-enabled workspace solutions provider. We operate 85 locations across 12 cities, serving over 50,000 members through flexible workspace memberships. Our mission is to elevate human consciousness through collaborative work environments.

We are a "controlled company" within the meaning of the NYSE listing standards. As a result, we are exempt from the requirements that a majority of our board of directors be independent, that we have a compensation committee composed entirely of independent directors, and that we have a nominating and corporate governance committee composed entirely of independent directors.

RISK FACTORS

We have a history of losses and may not achieve profitability. We incurred net losses of $45 million, $85 million, and $120 million in 2021, 2022, and 2023, respectively. Our losses have been increasing as we expand into new markets.

We depend on our founder and CEO. John Founder serves as our Chief Executive Officer, Chairman of the Board, and controls approximately 72% of the voting power of our outstanding capital stock through his ownership of Class B common stock, which carries 20 votes per share compared to 1 vote per share for Class A common stock.

We consolidate AsiaCo as a variable interest entity (VIE). We do not hold a majority equity interest in AsiaCo, but we consolidate its results based on contractual arrangements that give us the power to direct its activities. If regulatory changes in the relevant jurisdictions challenge VIE structures, we could lose access to AsiaCo's revenue and assets while remaining liable for associated obligations.

RELATED PARTY TRANSACTIONS

John Founder, our CEO, personally owns two commercial properties that are leased to MiniCorp. The aggregate future minimum lease payments under these related-party leases total approximately $10.3 million over the remaining lease terms of 12 years. These leases were entered into at rates that management believes approximate fair market value at the time of execution.

In 2023, WF Holdings LLC, an entity controlled by John Founder, sold the trademark rights to the name "Mini" to MiniCorp for consideration of $2.0 million in Class A common stock. John Founder registered the "Mini" trademark in 2019 through WF Holdings LLC.

The Company has issued a promissory note to John Founder in the aggregate principal amount of $50 million. The note bears interest at a rate of 1.5% per annum and is secured by Mr. Founder's Class B shares. Proceeds were used by Mr. Founder for personal real estate investments, including properties subsequently leased back to MiniCorp.

Sarah Founder, spouse of John Founder, serves as Chief Brand Officer of MiniCorp and as CEO of MiniGrow, our education subsidiary. Ms. Founder receives annual compensation of $1.2 million. MiniGrow has generated no revenue since its founding in 2021.

EXECUTIVE COMPENSATION

In fiscal year 2023, John Founder was granted 15,000,000 stock options at an exercise price of $12.00. The total option pool for 2023 was 18,500,000 shares, meaning Mr. Founder received approximately 81% of all options granted during the fiscal year.

USE OF PROCEEDS

We estimate the net proceeds from this offering will be approximately $500 million. We intend to use approximately $200 million to repay outstanding indebtedness, including the $50 million promissory note held by John Founder. The remainder will be used for general corporate purposes and expansion.

CORPORATE STRUCTURE

In addition to our Chief Executive Officer role, John Founder serves as landlord to MiniCorp for two properties, as a creditor through the $50 million promissory note, as a trademark licensor through WF Holdings LLC, and as the controlling stockholder through Class B super-voting shares. If the succession plan is triggered, a three-person committee including Sarah Founder will select the next CEO.

NON-GAAP FINANCIAL MEASURES

We present "Community Adjusted EBITDA" which we define as net income (loss) excluding: interest expense, income taxes, depreciation and amortization, stock-based compensation expense, general and administrative expenses, pre-opening location costs, and restructuring charges. For fiscal year 2023, our Community Adjusted EBITDA was positive $18 million, compared to our GAAP net loss of $120 million.
