FORM 10-K ANNUAL REPORT

BurnCo Inc.

For the fiscal year ended December 31, 2023

BUSINESS OVERVIEW

BurnCo Inc. ("BurnCo" or the "Company") is a cloud-based enterprise analytics platform. We serve over 2,400 customers across 30 countries. Our platform processes over 500 billion data events per month. We were founded in 2017 and have grown rapidly through organic expansion and strategic acquisitions.

In March 2023, we acquired DataMesh Corp for $85 million in cash and stock. DataMesh contributed approximately $8 million in revenue for the period from acquisition to year-end.

RISK FACTORS

We have incurred significant losses since inception and may never achieve profitability. We incurred net losses of $55 million, $78 million, and $110 million in fiscal years 2021, 2022, and 2023, respectively. Our accumulated deficit as of December 31, 2023 was $310 million.

We depend on a limited number of large customers. For fiscal year 2023, MegaBank Corp accounted for approximately 19% of our total revenue ($15.2 million). The loss of MegaBank Corp or a significant reduction in their usage would materially impact our results.

We may not have sufficient cash to fund operations. As of December 31, 2023, we had $45 million in cash and cash equivalents. Our net cash used in operating activities was $90 million in fiscal 2023. At our current rate of cash consumption, our existing cash will be sufficient to fund operations for approximately 6 months. We will need to raise additional capital through equity or debt financing. There can be no assurance that such financing will be available on acceptable terms, if at all. If we are unable to raise additional capital, there is substantial doubt about our ability to continue as a going concern.

Our convertible notes mature in 2025, creating significant refinancing risk. We have $200 million in aggregate principal amount of 3.5% convertible senior notes due June 2025. If we cannot refinance or repay these notes at maturity, we may be forced to pursue a restructuring.

NON-GAAP FINANCIAL MEASURES

We present "Adjusted EBITDA" which we define as net loss excluding: interest expense, income tax provision, depreciation and amortization ($18 million), stock-based compensation expense ($30 million), and restructuring and other charges ($12 million). For fiscal year 2023, our Adjusted EBITDA was positive $20 million, compared to our GAAP net loss of $110 million.

We believe Adjusted EBITDA is useful because it allows investors to evaluate our operating performance without the impact of non-cash and non-recurring items.

Accounts Receivable Aging

Accounts receivable increased from $15.5 million at December 31, 2022 to $28.0 million at December 31, 2023, an increase of 81%. Revenue grew 25% over the same period. Days sales outstanding (DSO) increased from 88 days to 128 days. Management believes the increase reflects the shift toward larger enterprise customers with longer payment cycles.

DEBT AND FINANCING

Convertible Notes

In June 2020, we issued $200 million aggregate principal amount of 3.5% Convertible Senior Notes due June 2025. The notes are convertible into shares of common stock at a conversion price of $25.00 per share, representing 8,000,000 potential shares. As of December 31, 2023, our common stock was trading at $12.50 per share.

Term Loan

In December 2021, we entered into a $50 million term loan with Pacific National Bank. The loan matures in December 2026 and bears interest at SOFR + 4.5%. The term loan contains financial covenants requiring us to maintain (i) minimum annual revenue of $60 million and (ii) a total leverage ratio of no more than 5.0x trailing twelve-month revenue.

UNIT ECONOMICS

As of December 31, 2023, we had 2,400 customers generating average annual revenue of $33,333 per customer. Our customer acquisition cost (CAC) for new customers acquired in 2023 was approximately $85,000. Our average customer lifetime is estimated at 3.2 years, implying a lifetime value (LTV) of approximately $107,000 and an LTV/CAC ratio of 1.26x.

Net revenue retention rate was 108% for the twelve months ended December 31, 2023, down from 115% in fiscal 2022.

VALUATION AND FUNDING HISTORY

In September 2022, GrowthCapital Partners led our Series E financing round, purchasing 50,000,000 shares of Series E preferred stock at $40.00 per share for aggregate proceeds of $2.0 billion in primary capital. This transaction established a post-money valuation of $8.0 billion. No public market price discovery was involved; the valuation was based on a proprietary discounted revenue multiple model prepared by GrowthCapital's internal team. As of December 31, 2023, our common stock trades on NASDAQ at $12.50 per share, implying a market capitalization of approximately $1.25 billion.
